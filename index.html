Elastic lists are an interactive way to explore large datasets, allowing users to filter, search, and drill down into information seamlessly. They adapt dynamically to user selections, offering a flexible interface for data exploration.

Demo 1: Simple

Simple demo of elastic lists using Nobel Prize winners.
Demo 2: Count column

Includes filter header for columns and limits the shown columns.
Demo 3: Filter data

Allows for filtering of the columns for the Nobel Prize winners.
Demo 4: Grace

Demonstrates filtering of an ontology.

This concept is based on the work of Moritz Stefaner, whose research and projects explore the principles of dynamic, interactive visualizations. For more on his work with elastic lists, you can refer to these papers:

Stefaner, M., Urban, T., & Seefelder, M. (2008, September). Elastic lists for facet browsing and resource analysis in the enterprise. In 2008 19th International Workshop on Database and Expert Systems Applications (pp. 397-401). IEEE.

Stefaner, M., & Muller, B. (2007, September). Elastic lists for facet browsers. In 18th International Workshop on Database and Expert Systems Applications (DEXA 2007) (pp. 217-221). IEEE.
